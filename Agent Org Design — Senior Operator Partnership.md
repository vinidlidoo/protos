# Agent Org Design — a senior-operator partnership for redesigning F500 functions around AI agents

**Wedge:** 14-week single-function "Agent Org Redesign" for PE-portfolio CEOs and F500 COOs — ships a working multi-agent stack in client infra, a new org chart, and one certified Agent Ops Manager left behind to run it.

## TL;DR

PE-portfolio CEOs and F500 COOs pay $400–600K for a 14-week partner-led redesign of one function around AI agents — working multi-agent stack in their infra, new org chart, one trained Agent Ops Manager left behind. The engagement is the lead funnel for a four-stream product business: a free framework book, a software-only AOM certification, a workflow-pattern SaaS, a placement marketplace. Bet works if buyers pay function-scoped prices for a bundle Big-3 economics can't replicate and agent-product shops can't ship vendor-neutral.

## What is it?

Two senior operators take one function inside a $1–10B-revenue company — trend analytics, category mgmt, claims ops, market research — and over 14 weeks redesign it around AI agents. They ship a working multi-agent stack inside client infrastructure, rewrite the org chart, and train a dedicated **Agent Ops Manager** (often someone already inside) certified through our curriculum to run the system after we leave.

The buyer is the COO, CHRO, or PE operating partner. They get a function that ships faster at lower headcount with one accountable human in charge — not a slide deck, not a pilot that dies in IT review. By year 2 the engagement is the lead funnel for four product streams (see *How it could make money*): a methodology business with software margins on three of four lines, not a consulting firm.

None bundles vendor-neutral redesign + working stack + placed-and-certified operator in one quarter. The seam between "we have a model" and "this function now runs differently" is un-owned.

## Customer & buyer

- **Customer:** VP/SVP of Category Mgmt, Trend Analytics, Claims Ops, Market Research, or Competitive Intel; $1–10B revenue; 10–60 knowledge workers.
- **Buyer:** COO, CHRO, or PE-portfolio CEO under VCP pressure. Budget: org transformation / opex (not IT). ACV $400–600K per engagement; sequenced → $1.2–1.8M annual.
- **Champion:** the function leader (Customer above) — sells up to the COO.
- **ICP test:** (a) board named AI top-3 in writing, (b) bounded P&L or output metric, (c) internal AOM candidate nameable in 30 days, (d) PE-owned or PE-style governance preferred.
- **Anti-customer:** "AI strategy slides" shoppers; anyone insisting the deliverable belongs to IT; anyone unwilling to name an internal AOM candidate before kickoff.

## Wedge / where we could enter

The narrowest first move is the 14-week single-function engagement at $400–600K with the placed AOM as non-negotiable third deliverable. Three live segments:

- **A — PE portfolio companies, years 2–3 of hold.** Operating partner unlocks budget; metric is 12-month EBITDA. Warmest channel via INSEAD. Fastest cycle (60–90 days).
- **B — F500 trend analytics / category mgmt / market research.** Vincent's Amazon turf; best for technical credibility on engagement #1.
- **C — F500 claims / underwriting.** Highest WTP (regulated, measurable error rates) but longest cycle; needs a payer/PBM domain co-conspirator.

Default ranking: A → B → C unless the experiment surprises us.

## What makes it defensible

- **(a) Primary — Methodology IP and category vocabulary.** Vincent ships the *Agent Org Design Framework* in year 1: free book + paid certification. Naming roles, maturity stages, assessment rubric is the moat (Lean Startup, Spotify Squads, Scrum). Whoever defines the vocabulary owns the conversation.
- **(b) Secondary — Placed-talent flywheel.** Each engagement seeds a certified AOM inside an F500 or portco. After ~20 placements, "Agent Ops Manager" means "the cert from us"; referrals + demo sites + recruiter pipeline + marketplace supply (L4) compound from the same node.
- **(c) Year-3 endgame — three product lines at SaaS margins.** Cert (L2), library (L3), marketplace (L4) carry zero partner-time per dollar. Services (L1) is lead-gen + R&D, not the profit engine.

## How it could make money

Four streams. L1 is the lead funnel; L2–L4 carry SaaS-shaped margins.

| Layer | Price range | Comparable |
|---|---|---|
| **L1 — Partner-led engagement** | $400–600K / 14 wks / function | [Mid-tier AI consultancy $100–500K bracket](https://xcelacore.com/ai-consulting-pricing-for-companies/); [boutique $150–300/hr](https://www.groovyweb.co/blog/ai-consulting-rates-2026) × ~1,400 partner-hrs |
| **L2 — AOM certification** (software-only) | $25–40K / certified seat | [Section cohorts](https://www.sectionai.com/courses/generative-ai-business-strategy); [Workera enterprise](https://www.workera.ai/) |
| **L3 — Workflow library SaaS** | $150–300K / yr / portfolio or division | [Galileo](https://galileo.ai/), [Patronus](https://www.patronus.ai/) eval-platform tiers |
| **L4 — Placement marketplace** | 20–25% of AOM first-year base ($30–50K) | [Tech-recruiter contingent 20–30%](https://www.recruiterslineup.com/contingency-recruiting-fee-structure/) |

Pricing-math: $500K / 14 wks ÷ 2 partners ≈ $18K/partner-week, vs $25–40K/wk standard senior consultant rate — priced 25–50% under to win share; margin from L2–L4. **L1 is a loss leader for the product business by year 2** — explicit plan, the named guard against the senior-boutique trap.

## What would pressure the thesis

1. **Assumption:** Buyers pay $400–600K for function-scoped redesign rather than a $5M McKinsey program or hiring two ex-McKinsey alums.
   - **Pressure:** <4 of 12 say "we'd pay $400K with the screencast shown."
   - **Lever:** drop to a $150–250K diagnostic-only wedge (4 wks, no placement).
2. **Assumption:** A trained AOM is productive enough in 14 weeks; placement is a real deliverable.
   - **Pressure:** 0 of 2 candidates ship a basic multi-agent workflow in 5 days.
   - **Lever:** 6-wk pre-engagement bootcamp paid by the hiring company; tighter ICP.
3. **Assumption:** L2–L4 compound fast enough that L1 isn't the profit engine.
   - **Pressure:** by month 18, cert revenue <$500K ARR and library has zero paying customers.
   - **Lever:** kill L4, consolidate on cert + library; admit this is a 3-partner shop.

## 2-week experiment

**Load-bearing assumption:** PE-portfolio CEOs and F500 COOs commit budget *in writing* for a $400–600K function-scoped engagement *when shown a working multi-agent stack producing a real output for a function they recognize* — not "send a proposal."

**Artifact (week 1, ~30 hrs):** Multi-agent trend-analytics stack for a fictional CPG brand, end-to-end via Claude Code + 3–4 subagents. Output: ~10-page competitive-intel + trend report on a fictional skincare line going into Q3 — charts, sourced quotes, assortment recommendation. 5-min screencast hosted on Loom + LinkedIn + GitHub.

**Conversations (week 2, ~25 hrs):** 12 30-min calls; every call opens with the screencast.

- 7 PE-portfolio CEOs (years 2–3 of hold) via INSEAD + Vincent's network
- 4 F500 trend analytics / category mgmt / market research VPs
- 1 F500 claims / underwriting leader — artifact-transfer signal

**Questions** (while the buyer is looking at the artifact):

1. If we shipped this for *your* function in 14 wks on *your* infra, what would you pay?
2. Who internally is the AOM candidate? (No name = kill account.)
3. Worst outcome you've had with an AI consulting engagement?

Side experiment (~10 hrs): 2 candidates attempt the AOM curriculum — can either ship a basic multi-agent workflow in 5 days?

**Pass (all three):** ≥4 of 12 verbal "yes I'd pay $400K+" with named internal AOM candidate; ≥1 buyer pulls a Q3 2026 PO date; screencast hits ≥1,500 LinkedIn views or ≥10 inbound replies in 7 days.

**Kill (any one):** ≤2 of 12 verbal yes; 0 of 2 candidates ship the curriculum; screencast flat (<200 views, <2 inbound).

**Vincent's effort:** ~65 hrs (≈30 build, ≈25 conversations, ≈10 curriculum). Output: public screencast + repo + 5-page memo with quoted answers, named accounts, go/pivot/kill. If go: 1 LOI for a 50%-discounted first pilot ($200–300K).
